Nepali Language

Vowels, syllables, and numbers

The building blocks of Nepali language are: 12 vowels, 36 root syllables and decimal numerical system. We will explore the numerical system later in the lessons. Our primary focus for some lessons will be letters, words, phrases, and sentences.

Click the sections below for more details on vowels, syllables, and conjugated syllables that are the main building blocks of Nepali language.

Conjugated Syllables Description

Conjugated Syllables

Pay particular attention to the making of the root syllables. They are composed of vowels and root consonants. For example, क (ka) is made up of क् (k) ‌+ अ (a). Same goes with other root syllables.

Each of the 36 primary syllables have 12 conjugated syllables. These conjugated syllables are made up of primary consonant of each primary syllable and combined with 12 vowels. Examples: क् ‌+ अ = क; ख् + आ ‍= खा, etc..

Ultimately, each of the root syllables are expanded into 12 conjugated syllables depending on which vowels they are combined with. This means, once you know how to compose 12-iterations of one root syllables, you will be able to do that for all others.

You might want to print these vowels and root syllables and put it up on your wall by your computer.

Once you learn basic characters, words and sentences, you will be able to enhance your Nepali language competence by regular practice of writing, reading, talking, and reading out loud.
